About The Workshop

About The Workshop

The 1st International Workshop on Intelligent IoT Systems for Extreme Environments (IoT4X 2025) focuses on the design, development, and deployment of intelligent IoT systems in extreme environments. These environments include harsh climates (e.g., arid deserts, polar regions), subsea exploration, space missions, disaster-stricken areas, and remote industrial sites, where traditional IoT systems struggle due to severe physical, environmental, communication and computation constraints. The workshop will bring together researchers, industry practitioners, and experts to explore innovative solutions for IoT systems that can operate effectively in these challenging settings.

Event Venue

Hakodate City, Hokkaido, Japan

Hakodate is one of the main cities on Hokkaido, Japan's northernmost island. Looming over the town is 334m-high Mount Hakodate. The mountain's summit, reachable by aerial ropeway gondola, offers dramatic nighttime views. At its base is Motomachi, a neighborhood of steep streets with Western-style, early-20th-century buildings. A city landmark is the grand Old Public Hall of Hakodate Ward, built in 1910.

Topics

Possible topics include, but are not limited to

  • Intelligent sensing and edge computing in extreme environments
  • AI/ML algorithms for IoT in constrained conditions
  • Resilient IoT architectures and protocols
  • Goal-orinted semantic communicaiton system design for constrained environments
  • Energy-efficient and self-sustaining IoT systems
  • Fault-tolerant and self-healing IoT networks
  • Robust communication in low-connectivity or noisy environments
  • Hardware design for remote deployments
  • IoT for disaster response and emergency management
  • Environmental monitoring in polar, desert, or oceanic zones
  • Autonomous decision-making under uncertainty
  • Reinforcement learning for real-time control in hostile conditions
  • AI-driven anomaly detection in sensor networks
  • Field-deployable testbeds in remote/extreme locations
  • Simulation tools for harsh environments
  • Digital twins enabling interoperability between real-world systems and virtual models
  • Case studies from real-world deployments
  • Data sets and benchmarks for intelligent IoT in harsh contexts
  • Cybersecurity for IoT in critical infrastructures

Submission

Submission Instructions

Full Paper (4-6 pages)
Authors are invited to submit their original research work that has not previously been submitted or published in any other venue. Workshop session papers need to be submitted in IEEE CS Proceedings format. IEEE formatting info can be found here.
All the accepted papers will be published by IEEE in the Conference Proceedings (IEEE-DL and EI indexed). Best Paper Awards will be presented to high-quality papers. Selected papers will be recommended to prestigious journal Special Issues.
Some papers originally submitted as full papers can be accepted as short papers during the review process. In such cases, the authors will need to reduce the paper accordingly when preparing the camera-ready version. At least one of the authors of any accepted paper is requested to register and present the paper at the conference.
  • Paper Submission: June 20, 2025 July 10, 2025
  • Acceptance Notification: June 30, 2025
  • Camera-ready Submission: Sept 12, 2025
Please note that for the camera-ready version, the page limit is 6 pages. However, a maximum of two extra pages per paper is allowed (i.e., up to 8 pages), at an additional charge of $100 per extra page.
We refer the authors to the conference website for detailed and updated instructions.
All papers will be peer-reviewed by at least two members of the program committee. Accepted papers will be part of the conference proceedings.
Papers need to be submitted via EDAS (https//edas.info/N33777) and select IOT4X2025 Track.
